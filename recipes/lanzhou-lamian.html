Lanzhou lamian

Description

Lanzhou lamian is a Chinese noodle dish that's known for its hand pulled noodles as the dough is stretched and folded repeatedly by hand to create long, thin noodles with a chewy texture.

The special technique that is used not only aligns the gluten but also enhances the texture of the noodles. The noodles are then cooked in a rich beef broth that is clear and flavourful, served with sliced beef and granished with cilantro, chilli oil and white radish with the option of other garnishes available

Indgredients

  • 250g all purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 125 ml water
  • cooking oil to coat the dough
  • Chinese chili oil
  • 250g beef
  • 2 beef bones
  • 200g white radish
  • chopped coriander

Steps

  1. Make a smooth, slightly firm dough using flour, salt and water.
  2. Divide then flatten the dough. Coat with oil then leave to rest.
  3. Bring a pot of water to boil and cut the dough into thin strips.
  4. Pull the strips into long noodles one at a time and then proceed to drop into the water.
  5. Cook for 2-3 minutes and dish out
  6. Put beed chunks and bones into a pot filled cold water. Bring the water to a full boil, Use a spoon to skim off the froth on the surface
  7. Add in all the herbs and spices and leave to simmer till beef is cooked through(2 hours)
  8. Take the beed out to cool and discard the bones, herbs and spices.
  9. When the beef is cold cut into thin slices.
  10. Cut radish into thin slices and cook in beef broth until soft(15 minutes).
  11. Cook noodles in another pot with water and drain and rinse under cold water.
  12. Place noodles in seving bowls. Pour in hot broth and radish.
  13. Top with beef and granish with coriander and chili oil.
